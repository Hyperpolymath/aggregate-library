## Maintainer Responsibilities

Maintainers are responsible for:

- **Specification Review**: Reviewing and approving changes to operation specifications
- **Quality Assurance**: Ensuring specifications meet SPEC_FORMAT.md standards
- **Community Management**: Responding to issues, PRs, and community questions
- **Documentation**: Maintaining project documentation and guidelines
- **Release Management**: Coordinating releases and version updates
- **Security**: Addressing security reports and vulnerabilities
- **Governance**: Making decisions on project direction and scope

## Decision-Making Process

### Specification Changes

- **Minor Changes** (typos, clarifications): Single maintainer approval
- **Significant Changes** (new test cases, edge case additions): Single maintainer approval after community input period (3-7 days)
- **Major Changes** (new operations, breaking changes): Consensus among maintainers + community discussion (2-4 weeks)

### Consensus

For decisions requiring consensus:
1. Proposal is posted publicly (GitHub issue/discussion)
2. Community feedback period (minimum 7 days)
3. Maintainers discuss and reach agreement
4. Decision is documented and communicated

## Becoming a Maintainer

The aggregate-library project welcomes new maintainers who have:

### Criteria

- **Sustained Contributions**: Regular, high-quality contributions over 6+ months
- **Specification Expertise**: Deep understanding of specification writing and cross-language design
- **Community Engagement**: Helpful, respectful engagement with community members
- **Alignment with Values**: Demonstrated commitment to project values (emotional safety, inclusivity, quality)
- **Trust**: Earned trust of existing maintainers and community

### Process

1. **Nomination**: Existing maintainer nominates candidate (or self-nomination)
2. **Discussion**: Maintainers discuss candidate's contributions and fit
3. **Consensus**: All existing maintainers must approve
4. **Invitation**: Candidate is invited and onboarded
5. **Announcement**: New maintainer announced to community

### Onboarding

New maintainers receive:
- Repository commit access
- Introduction to governance processes
- Mentorship from experienced maintainer
- Addition to maintainer communication channels
- Update to this MAINTAINERS.md file

## Stepping Down

Maintainers may step down at any time by:

1. Notifying other maintainers
2. Completing handoff of responsibilities
3. Transitioning to emeritus status (if desired)
4. Updating this document

No explanation required. We respect personal decisions and life changes.

## Removal

In rare cases, a maintainer may be removed for:

- Prolonged inactivity (12+ months without response)
- Violation of Code of Conduct
- Breach of trust or abuse of access
- Consistent failure to fulfill responsibilities

**Process:**
1. Private discussion among other maintainers
2. Attempt to resolve concerns with maintainer
3. If unresolved, consensus decision to remove
4. Communication to removed maintainer
5. Update to this document and access revocation

## Maintainer Rotation

To prevent burnout and ensure sustainability:

- Maintainers are encouraged to take breaks as needed
- No minimum activity requirement (but communication expected)
- Temporary "inactive" status available for breaks
- Collective responsibility - work is distributed

## Contact

To reach maintainers:

- **Public**: Open a GitHub issue
- **Private**: Email maintainers at conduct@[project-domain]
- **Security**: Use SECURITY.md reporting process

## Transparency

All maintainer decisions are:
- Documented in issues, PRs, or discussion threads
- Made publicly when possible (private only for security/conduct issues)
- Open to community feedback and input
- Explained with clear reasoning

## Governance Evolution

This governance structure may evolve as the project grows. Proposed changes to maintainer structure or processes should be:

1. Proposed via GitHub issue/discussion
2. Open for community feedback (minimum 30 days)
3. Approved by consensus of current maintainers
4. Documented in this file with changelog
